Soft tissue:Postoperative changes are seen in the 4th and 5th fingers.Multispatial, ill-defined, lobulated, heterogeneous mass lesions are seen involving hypothenar and 2nd to 4th intermetacarpal regions of hand and extending to the dorsal aspect of the hand. Proximally the lesion is seen extending to the carpal tunnel region and distally upto the tips of 4th and 5th digits. The lesion is seen involving the subcutaneous and muscular planes in both palmar and dorsal aspects. The lesion is also involving the 3rd, 4th and 5th metacarpal shafts and phalanges of 4th and 5th fingers. Encasement of all flexor digitorum tendons is seen. Encasement of 3rd to 5th extensor tendons is seen. Involvement of 4th and 5th carpometacarpal joints and MCP and IP joints of 4th and 5th fingers are also seen.   The lesions appear T2/ IR hyperintense, T1 hypointense, and shows altered diffusivity. Post contrast images show heterogeneous enhancement in early phases and progressive homogenous enhancement in delayed images. Multiple blood fluid levels are seen within. No non enhancing cystic areas are seen.Rest of the soft tissue appears grossly normal. Bones and joints:Distal radioulnar, radiocarpal, ulnocarpal and other joints appear grossly normal for age. No other significant joint effusion or synovitis is seen.  Rest of the visualized bones appears normal in position, alignment and articulation. IMPRESSION: "  Multispacial, lobulated enhancing soft tissue mass lesion involving subcutaneous/ muscular/ osseous planes of palmar and dorsal aspects of hand with extensions and imaging characteristics as detailed.
